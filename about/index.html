About

My name is Zihan Huang, my journey of life started at a small town in northwestern China. After completing my undergraduate in Financial Engineering in Beijing, I made one of the most important decision in my life so far - transitioned into data analytics and came to the U.S. for graduate study during the covid times. As I finished my master’s program, I’m resolved to apply for a Ph.D. program in Decision Science/Operations Research/Business Analytics to devote myself to research work. It is also my aspiration to pursue a future career in academia, where I can contribute to the field through research, mentorship, and the dissemination of knowledge to the next generation of scholars. My technical skills include programming languages like R and Python, structured query language (SQL), data visualization tools like Tableau and PowerBI, statistical analysis software like SPSS and Minitab, and Microsoft Office suite. I’m especially interested in combining the principles of decision-making and optimization with the data-driven capabilities of machine learning models like adaptive decision strategies with reinforcement learning, predictive analytics for decision-making, and integrating machine learning techniques into optimization models to improve decision outcomes. I’m also interested in topics like healthcare operations management, sports analytics, non-linear optimization and large-scale optimization, text mining and natural language processing.
