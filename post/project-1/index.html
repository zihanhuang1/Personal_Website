Project I: Text Mining on Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 2 Movie Script

November 10, 2023

Project Date: September 2021 - December 2021

Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows Part 2 was the last episode of the legendary series. In this movie, Harry has finally grown into a steely lad on a mission to rid the world of evil, and the friends must search for the Horcruxes that keep the dastardly wizard immortal.

Our research objectives in the project included studying the overall sentimental change as the story develops, comparing the word and sentiment between the two centers of the story - Harry and Voldemort, comparing the sentiment between the movie and the book, analyzing through the movie reviews to find if the movie was too dark compared to previous series.

As we formatted the script to excel sheets, we used packages dplyr, tidyr, stringr (to clean and format texts), wordcloud, wordcloud2 (to creat wordcloud), tidytext (for sentimental analysis) and igraph (for network analysis) in R to performed our analysis.

Our Social Network map basically demonstrated the relationships between characters in the movie. The main characters, such as Harry, Ron, Hermione and Voldemort are usually in the center of one social circle. However, the map also has room to further improve, as some of the relationships are not accurate in the current map.

Comparing Harry and Voldemort’s narratives, we noticed that as the main character on the justice side, Harry also said a lot of negative words, but most of them were associated to destroying the Horcruxes and the dark lord himself. Also, Harry’s narrative included more positive words compared to Voldemort.

As for what’s being said to both characters, we can see that Harry mostly received positive or neutral words, and what’s being said most to Voldemort was ’lord’, which quite matches his figure in the movie.

Above is the sentiment change through the movie for both main characters. We can see that even for harry, his sentiment from his narratives had more negative parts. However, compared to Voldemort, Harry’s negative level was lower, as the most negative sentiment score for harry was close to -4, while for Voldemort it was nearly -12.

Next, we evaluated the overall sentiment for all the scripts through the movie. Under different sentiment lexicon, the script has different sentiment scores. However, both AFINN and Bing et al. lexicon showed a mostly negative scoring.
